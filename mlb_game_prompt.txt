CORE PHILOSOPHY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This model does not bet ERA. ERA is noise.
This model bets SIERA, xFIP, wRC+, exit velocity, Chase Rate, and Sweet Spot Rate.
The public prices off last week's box score.
We price off underlying process metrics — including ones most bettors have never heard of.
That gap is where the money lives across a 162-game season.

METRIC GLOSSARY (reference):
  SIERA     — Skill-Interactive ERA. Best single ERA estimator. Accounts for batted ball mix.
  xFIP      — FIP with HR rate normalized to league avg. Removes HR luck.
  CSW%      — Called Strikes + Whiffs %. Best in-game pitch quality proxy.
  SwStr%    — Swinging strike %. Measures pure bat-missing ability.
  Chase Rate — % of pitches outside zone the batter swings at. High chase = easier to K and suppress hits.
  Whiff %   — % of swings that miss. Pitcher whiff rate feeds K%; batter whiff rate feeds hit suppression.
  Sweet Spot Rate — % of batted balls at launch angle 8-32°. Predicts BABIP better than hard hit % alone.
  BABIP     — Batting avg on balls in play. High pitcher BABIP = bad luck → regression coming.
  OAA       — Outs Above Average. Team fielding quality. Affects hits allowed behind pitcher.
  FRV       — Framing Run Value. Catcher's ability to steal called strikes. Adds real Ks to pitcher total.
  IVB       — Induced Vertical Break. Spin-induced fastball rise. High IVB = hitters swing under it.
  VAA       — Vertical Approach Angle. Flatter angle (-4.1° or better) = harder to square up.
  Extension — Release distance from rubber (ft). Higher = less batter reaction time = more Ks.

EARLY SEASON PROTOCOL (April – May, fewer than 8 starts):
  - Blend current 2026 stats with 2025 full-season stats
  - Weight: (current starts / 8) × 2026 stat + (1 - current starts / 8) × 2025 stat
  - Flag any projection using blended stats with ⚠️ SMALL SAMPLE
  - As starts accumulate, weight shifts fully to 2026 by June

PHASE 5 — PITCHER MODEL (build projected runs allowed)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STEP 1: Blend SIERA and xFIP into composite ERA estimate
  Composite ERA = (0.60 × SIERA) + (0.40 × xFIP)
  Note: SIERA weighted higher — accounts for batted ball mix better

  Early season adjustment (< 8 starts):
  Composite ERA = (starts/8 × 2026 Composite) + ((8-starts)/8 × 2025 Composite)
  Flag with ⚠️ SMALL SAMPLE if blended

  BABIP REGRESSION SIGNAL (apply after composite ERA):
  Compare pitcher's current BABIP allowed vs league avg (~.295-.305):
    BABIP > .330: pitcher has been unlucky → ERA inflated above true talent
      Signal: ERA likely to drop → lean UNDER on runs if SIERA gap is ≥ 0.50
      Flag: ⚠️ BABIP REGRESSION — ERA overstated, SIERA more predictive
    BABIP < .270: pitcher has been lucky → ERA suppressed below true talent
      Signal: ERA likely to rise → lean OVER on runs if gap is ≥ 0.50
      Flag: ⚠️ BABIP LUCK — ERA understated, regression risk
    BABIP .270-.330: neutral, no regression flag needed

STEP 2: Adjust for opponent offense (USE HANDEDNESS SPLITS)
  Pull team wRC+ vs pitcher hand (LHP or RHP):
    Opponent Offensive Adjustment = (team wRC+ vs pitcher hand) / 100
    Adjusted ERA = Composite ERA × Opponent Offensive Adjustment

  Also pull pitcher's own splits vs opposing lineup hand:
    Pitcher SIERA vs LHB and vs RHB (from Fangraphs splits tab)
    Pitcher K% vs LHB and vs RHB
    Pitcher BB% vs LHB and vs RHB
    If pitcher has a severe platoon split (>0.50 ERA diff vs hand):
      Use hand-specific SIERA instead of overall SIERA
      Flag: ⚠️ PLATOON SPLIT — pitcher notably worse vs [LHB/RHB]

  Lineup composition check:
    Count LHB vs RHB in projected starting lineup (1-9)
    If 6+ batters share handedness advantage vs pitcher: note as LINEUP EDGE

STEP 3: Apply park factor
  Park Adjusted ERA = Adjusted ERA × (park factor / 100)

STEP 3b: Apply team defense adjustment (OAA from Phase 4)
  Defense behind the pitcher affects BABIP → runs allowed
  Elite defense (OAA ≥ +20):  Park Adj ERA × 0.95  (saves ~0.15-0.20 ERA)
  Good defense (OAA +10 to +19): Park Adj ERA × 0.97
  Average defense (OAA -9 to +9): no adjustment
  Poor defense (OAA -10 to -19): Park Adj ERA × 1.03
  Bad defense (OAA ≤ -20):    Park Adj ERA × 1.05  (costs ~0.15-0.20 ERA)
  Defense Adjusted ERA = Park Adj ERA × defense multiplier

STEP 3c: Apply catcher framing adjustment (FRV from Phase 3)
  Elite framing catcher (FRV ≥ +8):  Defense Adj ERA × 0.97  (-0.10 to -0.15 ERA)
  Average framing (FRV -7 to +7):    no adjustment
  Poor framing catcher (FRV ≤ -8):   Defense Adj ERA × 1.03  (+0.10 to +0.15 ERA)
  Framing Adjusted ERA = Defense Adj ERA × framing multiplier
  (Use Framing Adjusted ERA as input for Steps 4-6)

STEP 4: Apply weather adjustment (from Phase 1)
  Weather ERA adjustment: for every +1 run weather adds to total,
  split evenly between both pitchers

STEP 5: Project innings pitched
  Expected IP = min(6.0, historical avg IP per start)
  For short-rest starters: cap at 5.0 IP

STEP 6: Project runs allowed by starter
  Starter Runs = (Park Adjusted ERA / 9) × Expected IP

STEP 7: Project bullpen runs
  Bullpen IP = 9 - Expected IP (starter)
  Bullpen ERA estimate = pull team bullpen ERA last 7 days
  Bullpen Runs = (Bullpen ERA / 9) × Bullpen IP

STEP 8: Total projected runs allowed
  Projected Runs Allowed = Starter Runs + Bullpen Runs
