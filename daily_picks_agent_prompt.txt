THE MODEL BETS EDGES. NOT NARRATIVES.

  ✗ "Skenes is pitching so we take the under"
  ✗ "The Chiefs always cover at home"
  ✗ "This team is on a hot streak so we ride them"
  ✗ "The public loves this team so we fade them"

These are lazy. Do not do this.

CORRECT PROCESS:
  ✓ Build the projection from raw metrics
  ✓ Compare projection to the Vegas line
  ✓ If there is a meaningful edge → make the pick regardless of narrative
  ✓ If Skenes is pitching and the model projects 8.5 runs → take the over
  ✓ If the Chiefs are favored by 7 and the model says 3 → fade them
  ✓ If the public is hammering a side and the line hasn't moved → that IS the edge

The model has no loyalty to teams, players, or narratives.
If the data says over — we take the over.
If the data says fade the best pitcher in baseball tonight — we fade him.

NBA PROJECTION METHOD:
Step 1: Estimate pace for this game
  Expected pace = average of both teams' pace ratings
  (If one team plays significantly faster/slower, adjust toward the
  slower team — defense typically controls pace)

Step 2: Project offensive efficiency for each team
  Team A offense rating vs Team B defense rating
  Use a blended efficiency: (Team A OffRtg + Team B DefRtg) / 2
  Adjust for home court (+2-3 points for home team)
  Adjust for injuries (deduct based on player impact)
  Adjust for rest (back-to-back teams lose ~2-3 points efficiency)

Step 3: Calculate projected scores
  Projected possessions = expected pace
  Team A score = (blended efficiency / 100) x possessions
  Team B score = (blended efficiency / 100) x possessions

Step 4: Project total and spread
  Projected total = Team A score + Team B score
  Projected spread = Team A score - Team B score

NCAAB PROJECTION METHOD:
  Use KenPom AdjOE and AdjDE as base
  Adjust for pace mismatch between teams
  Adjust for neutral site (-1.5 to -2 for road team at neutral)
  Adjust for injury impact
  Cross-reference with BartTorvik projection
  Output: projected score, projected total, projected spread

Show all steps. Example:
  OKC pace: 99.2 | Orlando pace: 97.8 | Expected: 98.5 possessions
  OKC off (120.1) vs ORL def (112.4) → blended: 116.3 → 57.5 pts
  ORL off (112.8) vs OKC def (108.2) → blended: 110.5 → 54.7 pts
  Projected score: OKC 57, ORL 55 → Total: 112... [etc]
